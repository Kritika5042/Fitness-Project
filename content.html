Key Areas of My Fitness Routine

Nutrition & Hydration

70% of your fitness journey is about what you eat. It's okay if you can't make it to the gym 4 times a week, but watching your diet is crucial.

  • High-Protein Diet: Prioritize protein-rich foods like Chicken, Eggs, Greek yogurt, Cottage Cheese, Tofu, and Lentils. Protein keeps you satiated for a longer time, helping you maintain a lower calorie intake while keeping you fuller longer. Pair this with high fiber foods
  • Carbs Matter: Fuel your body with nutritious carbs like rice, chickpea pasta, or sourdough bread instead of processed wheat-based options.
  • Craving Sweets? Opt for natural sugar sources like dates, watermelon, or any fruit of your choice. You can never overeat fruits!
  • Hydration: Drinking at least 3 liters of water daily to stay energized and support metabolism.

Walking

Daily Goal: 10,000+ steps to improve cardiovascular health and endurance.

Benefits: Burns calories, strengthens joints, and improves mental well-being.

Tips: Walk after meals to aid digestion and include incline walking for better fat burn.

Strength Training

You can still lose weight by staying active without strength training. However, if your goal is to achieve a more toned physique, incorporating strength training can help shape and define your body

4-Day Workout Split:

  • Day 1: Back & Biceps – Pull-ups, rows, face pull, curls.

  • Day 2: Shoulders & Chest – Overhead press, bench press, push-ups.

  • Day 3: Glutes & Hamstrings – Squats, RDLs, Hip Thrusts, Good Morning

  • Day 4: Quads & Calves – Leg press, lunges, calf raises, bulgarian spilt squat

    Importance: Builds muscle, boosts metabolism, and enhances strength

    • Recovery & Sleep

    Sleep Duration: 7-9 hours per night for optimal recovery.

    Muscle Recovery: Active stretching, mobility work, and foam rolling.

    Rest Days: Listening to your body and incorporating low-intensity activities like yoga or walking.
